Retracted Academia Collaborations

Identifying networks in retracted authorship

NETWORK ANALYSIS

Here you can play around with a force directed network of all the authors who were retracted due to a publishing banMay 25th, 2024

There are many reasons why a paper could be retracted. This project, however, is particularly looking at those authors, who were retracted and received a publishingban. A publishingban means, that either a journal or a commitee decided that these authors should not be able to publish their research due to serious issues with it. This graph shows, which networks can be identified in this group of banned researches and how interconnected they are. Nodes represent authors, edges show who has collaborated on an article. You can identify smaller, exclusive networks as well as filter by journal. The force directed graph will refresh the distances if you move a node or filter by journal. If you want to find the most important persons in this graph, try to find "Mori" or "Majid" and compare their individual networks. You can also see how different the topics of retracted articles can be by going through the journal names, however, there is a focus on natural sciences and medical articles, especially in this category of publishingban.

MAPPING RETRACTED NETWORKS

Take a look at some of the networks that span across the globe. Here you can find different authors and zoom into their connections.May 25th, 2024

This map gives an overview of connections between retracted authors worldwide. You can filter by journals or country and see how the collaborative networks are interconnected on an international level. This map is hosted by one of the contributers of the project.

Additionally, there is another map related to a specific scandal available for data exploring. Particularly interesting to this project was a Chinese-Iranian papermill which was found out some years ago. Interestingly, in this case researchers were either giving each other fake-citations or collecting false or manipulated peer reviews. In this leaflet map, nodes represent authors and the articles are each assigned to a unique colour to be able to differentiate between the connections. To be able to visualize the point of interconnectedness better, only a smaller portion of all the researches involved into the Chinese-Iranian papermill were visualized.
